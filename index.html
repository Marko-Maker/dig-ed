This site is an experiment in digital editing and annotating. It was cooperatively made by the students and teacher of English 107: Writing About Literature, a course taught at Pierce College, a community college in Lakewood, WA, during the Fall 2019 quarter.

In part, the goal of this project is to build and maintain an open pedagogical resource that helps students of literature better understand the historical, social, technological, philosophical, psychological, and cultural contexts for the literary genres of *speculative fiction*. For more on the orgins, rationale, and potential futures of this project, check out the about section and other documentation.

Below, you will find an initial collection of short works of speculative fiction from the early to mid 20th Century. As an open pedagogical project this work is very much in-progress in terms of goals, scope and quality. It follows the insight of open pedagogy practioners, like Abby L Goode, that open texts value both their publicness and potentially always "being far from finished products." The hope is that if you visit or use this site in a year, it will have evolved based on the as-yet-unknown goals and desires of it's makers and audience.

Get Started

To read the stories and view annotations select a story from the list and click "Annotate Me" from the sidebar menu. You may contribute your own annotations to the text by signing up for a hypothes.is account.
